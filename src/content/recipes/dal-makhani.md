## Ingredients

### To cook the dal
- 200 g black lentils / beluga lentils
- 1 tin red kidney beans

### Masala
- 30 ml ghee (or oil)
- 42 g unsalted butter
- 1 red onion, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
- 250 g tomato passata
- ¾ to 1½ tsp Kashmiri red chilli powder, to taste
- 1½ tsp garam masala (plus ½ tsp more to finish, optional)
- 1 tsp sea salt, to taste
- 500 ml hot water, added gradually
- 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
- 60–80 ml heavy cream

### Whole spices (optional)
- 1 bay leaf
- 2 green cardamoms
- 1 black cardamom

## Method

1. Heat the ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. If using the whole spices, add them now and let them sizzle for a minute.
2. Add the onion and cook until golden, 7–8 min. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute, until fragrant.
3. Pour in the tomato puree and cook for 3–4 min. Add the salt, chilli powder and garam masala, then keep cooking until the masala turns thick and smells rich, about 4–5 min.
4. Lightly mash the lentils and kidney beans, then tip them into the pot along with their liquid. Add 1 cup hot water and stir.
5. Simmer on the lowest possible heat, stirring often so nothing catches on the bottom. After about 30 min, add another ½–1 cup hot water and keep going. Aim for roughly 60-90 min of total simmering, until thick and creamy.
6. Add the fenugreek leaves and stir it in, then add the cream. Cook another 6–10 min. Taste and adjust salt and garam masala. Stir in the butter off the heat.
7. Serve hot with basmati rice or naan, as well as cilantro.
