## Ingredients

- 200 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), roughly chopped
- 30 g unsalted butter
- 4 large eggs, separated
- 2 tbsp caster sugar
- A pinch of fine salt
- A pinch of cream of tartar (or a few drops of lemon juice)

## Method

1. Melt the chocolate and butter together in a heatproof bowl set over a pan of barely simmering water (don't let the bowl touch the water). Stir until smooth, then remove from heat and let it cool for 5 minutes.

2. Whisk the egg yolks into the cooled chocolate one at a time, beating well after each addition. The mixture will thicken.

3. In a clean, grease-free bowl, beat the egg whites with the salt and cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Add the sugar and continue beating until you have firm, glossy peaks — they should hold their shape but still look smooth, not grainy.

4. Fold a large spoonful of the whites into the chocolate mixture to loosen it. Then gently fold in the remaining whites in two or three additions, using a large spatula with a light, scooping motion. Stop as soon as no white streaks remain — overmixing deflates the mousse.

5. Divide between small glasses or ramekins. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours (or overnight). Serve straight from the fridge — with a very small pinch of flaky salt on top if you like.
