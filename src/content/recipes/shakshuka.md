## Ingredients

- 4 large eggs
- 1 × 400 g tin of whole plum tomatoes
- 1 red bell pepper, roughly diced
- 1 yellow onion, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp sweet smoked paprika
- ½ tsp ground coriander
- A pinch of cayenne
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and black pepper
- A small bunch of fresh coriander or flat-leaf parsley
- Feta cheese, crumbled (optional but excellent)
- Crusty bread or flatbread, to serve

## Method

1. Heat olive oil in a wide, lidded pan over medium heat. Add the onion and pepper with a good pinch of salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 8–10 minutes until soft and lightly caramelised.

2. Add the garlic, cumin, paprika, ground coriander, and cayenne. Stir and fry for 1 minute until fragrant.

3. Pour in the tinned tomatoes, crushing them roughly with the back of a spoon. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer uncovered for 8 minutes, stirring now and then, until the sauce thickens slightly.

4. Make 4 small wells in the sauce with a spoon. Crack an egg into each well. Cover the pan and cook over medium-low heat for 5–7 minutes — the whites should be just set, yolks still runny. Check at 5 minutes.

5. Scatter over coriander or parsley and feta if using. Bring the pan straight to the table and serve with plenty of bread for scooping.
