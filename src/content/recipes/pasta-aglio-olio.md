## Ingredients

- 200 g spaghetti or linguine
- 6 cloves garlic, very thinly sliced
- 80 ml good extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp dried chilli flakes, or more to taste
- A large handful of flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
- Salt and black pepper
- Parmesan or Pecorino Romano, to serve (optional)

## Method

1. Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil. Cook pasta until 2 minutes shy of al dente — it will finish cooking in the sauce. Reserve a large mug (about 250 ml) of pasta water before draining.

2. While the pasta cooks, warm the olive oil in a wide pan over medium-low heat. Add the garlic slices and chilli flakes. Cook slowly, stirring often, for about 8 minutes — the garlic should turn pale golden and nutty, never brown. If it colours too fast, lift the pan off the heat.

3. Add half the pasta water to the garlic oil. It will hiss and sputter. Stir to combine.

4. Add the drained pasta and toss vigorously over medium heat, adding more pasta water a splash at a time, until the sauce is emulsified and clings to each strand — not oily, not watery.

5. Off heat, stir in the parsley. Season well with salt and black pepper. Serve immediately, with grated cheese if you like.
