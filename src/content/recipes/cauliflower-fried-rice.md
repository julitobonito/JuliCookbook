## Ingredients

- 1 small head cauliflower (about 600 g)
- 2 eggs, beaten (omit for vegan)
- 3 spring onions, sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cm ginger, minced
- 80 g frozen peas or edamame
- 2 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- White pepper, to taste

## Method

1. Cut the cauliflower into rough florets. Working in batches, pulse in a food processor until it resembles coarse rice grains — about 8–10 pulses. Don't over-process or it turns mushy. Alternatively, grate it on the large holes of a box grater.

2. Heat a large wok or wide pan over high heat until smoking hot. Add neutral oil. Add garlic, ginger, and the white parts of the spring onion. Stir-fry for 30 seconds.

3. Add the cauliflower in an even layer. Let it sit undisturbed for 60–90 seconds to get some colour on the bottom, then toss. Repeat until most of the moisture has cooked off and the cauliflower is lightly golden, about 5–6 minutes.

4. Add the peas or edamame and toss for 1 minute.

5. Push everything to the sides. Pour the beaten eggs into the centre of the wok and scramble them quickly, then fold them through the cauliflower rice before they fully set.

6. Add soy sauce and sesame oil. Toss well. Taste and adjust seasoning. Finish with white pepper and the green spring onion tops.
