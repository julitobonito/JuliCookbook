## Ingredients

- 450 g silken tofu, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 200 g ground pork (or beef)
- 2 tbsp doubanjiang (Sichuan chilli bean paste)
- 1 tbsp fermented black beans (douchi), roughly chopped
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- 250 ml chicken or pork stock
- 2 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp cold water
- 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns, toasted and ground
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 cm ginger, minced
- 3 spring onions, sliced (white and green parts separated)
- 3 tbsp neutral oil

## Method

1. Bring a pot of lightly salted water to a gentle simmer. Lower the tofu cubes in and poach for 3 minutes — this firms them up and keeps them from falling apart later. Drain carefully and set aside.

2. Heat oil in a wok over high heat until shimmering. Add the ground meat and cook, breaking it up, until browned and starting to crisp at the edges, about 4 minutes.

3. Reduce heat to medium. Push the meat to the side and add the doubanjiang, black beans, garlic, ginger, and white spring onion. Fry in the oil for 90 seconds — the paste should deepen in colour and smell intensely savoury.

4. Add Shaoxing wine, both soy sauces, and stock. Stir to combine and bring to a gentle boil.

5. Gently slide in the tofu cubes. Simmer for 3–4 minutes, carefully shaking the wok now and then rather than stirring, so the tofu stays whole.

6. Give the cornstarch mixture a stir and pour it in a little at a time, shaking the wok until the sauce coats the tofu in a glossy lacquer. You may not need all of it.

7. Pour into a bowl. Finish with ground Sichuan pepper and the green spring onion tops. Serve immediately over rice.
